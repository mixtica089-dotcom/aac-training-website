What is AAC?

Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) includes all forms of communication other than oral speech used to express thoughts, needs, wants, and ideas. It can include simple picture boards, communication books, or high-tech speech-generating devices.

Evidence-Based Teaching Strategies

  • Model AAC use throughout the day (Aided Language Input)
  • Incorporate AAC into routines and play
  • Use core vocabulary consistently
  • Encourage partner-assisted scanning and prompting
  • Collaborate with SLPs and families for device programming
